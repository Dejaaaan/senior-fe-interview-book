This book is a working reference for senior frontend engineers preparing for technical interviews. It is opinionated, code-first, and concentrated on the topics that genuinely appear in real loops: the language and platform foundations, modern React and Next.js, the Angular basics every senior is expected to recognise, frontend architecture and production concerns, Node.js backends in three flavours, Representational State Transfer (REST) and networking, authentication and authorisation, security and privacy, the core Amazon Web Services (AWS) surface that frontend engineers ship against, and a senior-level interview preparation toolkit.

It assumes that you have already shipped real products and are aiming for senior or staff-track roles. Where two patterns are in active use, the book takes a position and explains the trade-offs of the alternative rather than leaving the reader to choose between an undifferentiated list. This bias toward a recommendation is deliberate: in an interview the senior signal is often *which* approach you choose and *why*, not the ability to enumerate every approach in the literature.
