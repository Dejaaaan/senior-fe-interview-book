The previous parts have given the candidate the technical depth required for a senior frontend interview. This part teaches the candidate to express that depth in the interview itself. A typical senior-frontend interview loop comprises a recruiter screen (soft skills, expectations, compensation range); a hiring-manager conversation (the candidate's story, the role, the team); coding rounds (algorithms, framework specifics, often a "build me a small application" exercise); a frontend system-design round (the differentiator at the senior and staff level — most candidates pass or fail at this stage); a behavioural round ("tell me about a time when..."); and a bar-raiser, cross-functional, or leadership round at companies that follow the FAANG pattern.

The first three rounds are addressed by the technical material in [Part 2 (Foundations)](../02-foundations/index.md) through [Part 12 (AWS)](../12-aws/index.md). This part covers rounds four through six and the surrounding etiquette.

Each chapter ends with a "Common interview questions" section followed by an "Answers" section providing detailed model responses. These answers also serve as the canonical "model answers" for the worked system-design prompts and behavioural questions, so they are among the longest in the book.
